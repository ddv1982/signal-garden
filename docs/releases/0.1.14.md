# Release 0.1.14 — soften the moonlit cutouts

Polish the dark garden's companion, lens, and growth props so they read as moonlit objects in the scene instead of daylight stickers floating over a night backdrop.

## Highlights

- Replaces the broad dark-asset tint with profile-driven grading for companion frames, garden props, and individual lens props.
- Regenerates the existing dark runtime siblings without changing filenames, dimensions, anchors, texture keys, or hit targets.
- Reduces bright soft-edge halos on translucent lens art so clouds, pools, and ripples stop forming gray bubbles in dark mode.
- Adds dark-mode-specific Phaser lighting for lens glows, active rims, small glints, and pet grounding.
- Corrects the dark asset generator's contrast math to use a true midpoint adjustment after review and reference research.

## Lore

Constraint: Dark-mode assets should feel painted into the moonlit garden without moving any object, anchor, hit target, or saved-state contract.
Rejected: Regenerate the art from scratch | New image outputs could improve style, but they would raise consistency, framing, and QA risk for a polish release.
Rejected: Keep the blanket tint | It preserved dimensions, but it made the cat too daylight-bright and turned pale lenses into chalky cutouts.
Rejected: Hide the cutouts with one universal glow | The same circular glow made every lens sit inside a visible bubble instead of giving each object its own night affordance.
Rejected: Leave the first contrast formula implicit | Review showed it pulled midtones too aggressively, so the generator now uses an explicit midpoint adjustment.
Confidence: high
Scope-risk: medium
Directive: Keep the garden coordinate frame still, but let each dark asset and lens light carry its own moonlit treatment.
